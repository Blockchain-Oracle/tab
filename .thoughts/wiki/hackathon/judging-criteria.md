# Judging Criteria

> Source: `instuctions.md`. Different judges score different prizes — main tracks by
> the hackathon jury; subtracks & bonuses independently by the partner teams.

## Universal Accounts Track (flagship) — weighted
| Weight | Criterion |
|-------:|-----------|
| **40%** | **UX excellence** ← single biggest factor |
| **30%** | Prominent / innovative use of Universal Accounts + EIP-7702 |
| **20%** | Adoption potential |
| **10%** | Technical quality / polish |

**Read:** UX is king. Then "did you *really* lean on UA + 7702" (not a token integration).
Code polish is only 10% — judges care more that it *feels* seamless than that it's perfect.

## Arbitrum bounty — weighted
| Weight | Criterion |
|-------:|-----------|
| **30%** | UX excellence |
| **30%** | Creativity |
| **20%** | Adoption potential |
| **20%** | Execution quality |
- Tie-breaker: overall app quality.
- Tell: "The best projects will feel less like 'crypto apps' and more like normal consumer products that just happen to run onchain."

## Magic Labs bonus — qualitative
Judges look for: smooth onboarding/auth · creative use of Magic infra · UX polish & accessibility · consumer-ready product thinking · technical implementation quality.

## General Track
Judged on "exceptional user experience" — seamless, intuitive, genuinely improved by
the infrastructure. (Subtrack-specific judging handled by Openfort / ZeroDev.)

## Cross-cutting signal
Across **every** rubric, **UX is the highest or joint-highest weighted axis.** The
meta-strategy: a product that feels like a normal consumer app, with the sponsor tech
doing real (not cosmetic) work underneath.
